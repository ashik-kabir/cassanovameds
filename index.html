Emergency Protocol

What happens if something goes wrong in-country.

This is the protocol we brief every client on before departure. It is also the protocol we have used.

  • 01
    Pre-departure briefing

    Full medical history briefed to in-country coordinator. SafetyWing or Pacific Cross confirmed active. Preferred hospital pre-registered.

  • 02
    24-hour WhatsApp line

    Always answered. Not a call centre. Your coordinator's personal number — someone who knows your name and your history.

  • 03
    Coordinator goes to the hospital

    Not a referral. Not a phone number. Your Casanova coordinator is physically present — advocating, translating, and staying until you are stable.

  • 04
    Family communication

    Casanova handles all family-facing updates. You focus on recovery. We handle the logistics — extended stay, accommodation, visitor coordination.

Travel insurance, handled.

Frequently Asked

Honest answers.

Is Casanova a clinic or a medical provider?

No. Casanova is a medical navigation and patient advocacy company. All clinical care is performed by licensed physicians and institutions at our partner hospitals. We consult, suggest, route, and oversee — we do not diagnose, treat, or prescribe.

Do I need to travel to use Casanova?

No. CPP is fully remote — free intake, clinician review, and $249/month supplement protocol shipped to your door. In-country programs (The Reset, Restore, Renew) require travel to Thailand, Vietnam, or Malaysia.

What does the $4,500 / $9,500 / $18,500 cover?

Casanova's coordination fee. This covers all navigation, liaison, coordination, in-country check-ins, pre-trip physician consultation, post-trip care plan, and CPP enrollment. It does not include flights, accommodation, or clinical costs — which we itemize separately before you commit.

Is CPP a prescription service?

No. CPP is a clinician-reviewed OTC supplement program. Protocol A (AM) and Protocol B (PM) contain over-the-counter ingredients — Berberine, Magnesium Glycinate, L-Theanine, and others. A clinician reviews suitability before enrollment; no prescription is issued.

What if I'm not approved for CPP?

No subscription is charged. The clinician may suggest an alternative path or ask for more information. You will always receive a written explanation — the intake is never a black box.

Is this safe? Who are the doctors?

Bumrungrad International Hospital is rated among the top hospitals in Asia by international accreditation bodies. Our physician oversight partner holds active international clinical trial PI roles. We can share institutional credentials and COA documentation on request.

Can I do this on my own without Casanova?

Yes — and some people do. What Casanova offers is 10+ years of relationships, the ability to get appointments that aren't listed online, in-country coordination if something goes wrong, and a year-long care arc rather than a single trip.

What about my privacy and medical records?

Intake data is stored securely and used only for clinician review and program coordination. Outcomes data is de-identified for research purposes with your consent, captured via §07A structured tracking. We hold data to the same standard as a US-based patient advocate.
